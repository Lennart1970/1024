The main moral costs depend on context, but generally include:

- Late warnings about someone's character prevent prior harm

**The core issue:** Timing affects whether you're being honest versus merely confessing. There's a moral difference between difficult truth-telling and strategic revelation designed to minimize *your* consequences rather than serve the other person's interests.

The longer you wait, the more the moral failure shifts from the content of what you're saying to your choice to conceal it.
